Bookmarked (UW Capstone)

Name of Project: Bookmarked (UW Capstone)

Goals of Project: Build a full-stack social reading platform where users can rate, tag, organize, and discover books via social signals; validate product decisions through user research.

Your role in the project: Full-stack development and product design; designed relational schemas; led user research (25 respondents) to validate product decisions as part of Team JAAC.

Problem and/or opportunity: Readers lack a single place to track what they’ve read, share recommendations, and discover books through friends’ tastes. Social reading and organization tools are fragmented.

Your process and solution: Used React, TypeScript, Node.js, PostgreSQL, and Prisma to build the app; ran user research to prioritize features; designed and implemented the database schema and core flows for rating, tagging, and discovery.

Outcome and next steps: Capstone in progress. Next steps: launch MVP, gather feedback, and iterate on discovery and social features.

The takeaways: Importance of user research before scaling features; value of a clear relational model for social and recommendation data.

URL(s): Bookmarked does not have a public repository yet. Live demo and docs TBD as project launches.

SoccerSim

Name of Project: SoccerSim

Goals of Project: Build a large-scale football management and match simulation engine with probabilistic match logic, player progression, season simulations, and long-term data persistence.

Your role in the project: Design and implementation of the simulation engine, database schema, and performance benchmarking in TypeScript, Node.js, and PostgreSQL.

Problem and/or opportunity: Existing football sims are either too simplistic or closed-source. Opportunity to create a flexible, data-driven simulation with transparent logic and persistent state.

Your process and solution: Implemented probabilistic match logic and player progression; used PostgreSQL for persistence; ran performance benchmarks to support large-scale season simulations.

Outcome and next steps: Working simulation with season runs and data persistence. Next steps: richer tactics, more leagues, and optional UI for management gameplay.

The takeaways: Balancing simulation fidelity with performance; value of a clear data model for players, matches, and seasons.

URL(s): GitHub repo. Live demo and docs linked from repo if available.
